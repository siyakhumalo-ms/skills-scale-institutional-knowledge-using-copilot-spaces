# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, design intuitive user experiences, and validate design solutions. They collaborate with product managers, developers, and stakeholders to ensure that products are usable, accessible, and delightful.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design standards
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support tickets related to usability
- Ensure design consistency across products

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with product and engineering teams
- User research synthesis and presentation
- Collaboration with developers during implementation
- Work closely with Product Managers during feature definition
- Partner with Technical Writers on user-facing documentation

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain product documentation, user guides, API references, and internal process documentation. They ensure that information is clear, accurate, and accessible to the intended audience.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user-facing documentation and help content
- Create and update API documentation and developer guides
- Document internal processes and project workflows
- Collaborate with developers to understand technical features
- Review and improve documentation based on user feedback
- Ensure documentation consistency and adherence to style guides

### Goals
- Make products easier to use through clear documentation
- Reduce support burden by providing comprehensive self-service resources
- Maintain up-to-date and accurate technical content

### Typical Communication
- Collaborate with Developers and Product Managers on feature documentation
- Work with UX Designers to align documentation with user flows
- Participate in sprint planning to understand upcoming features
- Partner with Support teams to identify documentation gaps

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile/Scrum practices, remove impediments, and coach the team on continuous improvement. They ensure the team follows agreed processes and foster a culture of collaboration and self-organization.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, daily standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Remove blockers and impediments for the team
- Coach team members on Agile principles and practices
- Protect the team from external disruptions
- Track and visualize team progress and metrics
- Foster continuous improvement and team health

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Create an environment of psychological safety and collaboration
- Ensure adherence to Agile best practices

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members to identify impediments
- Coordination with Project Managers on cross-team dependencies
- Regular retrospectives to drive continuous improvement
- Work with Product Managers on backlog refinement

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment automation. They enable reliable, fast, and secure delivery of software to production environments.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Ensure security and compliance in deployment processes
- Support incident response and troubleshooting
- Optimize build times and deployment reliability

### Goals
- Minimize deployment friction and manual toil
- Ensure system reliability, security, and observability
- Enable fast, frequent, and safe releases

### Typical Communication
- Collaborate with Developers on build and deployment issues
- Work with Project Managers on release planning and timelines
- Partner with Security teams on compliance and vulnerability management
- Coordinate with Support teams during incidents
- Participate in sprint planning for infrastructure work

---

## Support/Customer Success Specialist

### Role Summary
Support and Customer Success Specialists help customers use products effectively, troubleshoot issues, and advocate for customer needs. They bridge the gap between customers and product/engineering teams.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to customer inquiries and support tickets
- Troubleshoot and resolve customer issues
- Document common issues and workarounds
- Escalate bugs and feature requests to product teams
- Provide product training and onboarding to customers
- Track customer health metrics and satisfaction

### Goals
- Maintain high customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce time to resolution for customer issues
- Provide actionable feedback to improve product quality

### Typical Communication
- Daily ticket triage and customer interactions
- Weekly syncs with Product Managers to share customer feedback
- Collaborate with Technical Writers on documentation improvements
- Work with Developers to understand and reproduce issues
- Partner with UX Designers to identify usability pain points

## Role Responsibility Matrix by Project Phase

This table provides a quick reference for understanding which roles are typically involved in each project phase and their level of engagement.

| Role | Initiation | Planning | Execution | Release | Retrospective |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **Product Manager** | Lead - Define problem statement, success metrics, stakeholder alignment | Lead - Prioritize backlog, define acceptance criteria | Active - Clarify requirements, validate solutions, make trade-off decisions | Active - Validate release readiness, communicate to stakeholders | Active - Review outcomes vs. goals, prioritize improvements |
| **Project Manager** | Lead - Create project charter, identify risks, establish timeline | Lead - Build project plan, manage dependencies, coordinate kickoff | Lead - Track progress, manage risks, facilitate meetings, report status | Lead - Coordinate deployment, ensure checklist completion | Lead - Facilitate retrospective, document learnings |
| **Developer** | Consult - Provide technical feasibility input, rough estimates | Active - Estimate work, identify technical risks and dependencies | Lead - Implement features, write tests, participate in code reviews | Active - Deploy code, monitor rollout, fix critical issues | Active - Share technical learnings, propose improvements |
| **UX Designer** | Active - Research user needs, validate problem space | Lead - Create designs, user flows, prototypes | Active - Support implementation, validate UI/UX fidelity | Consult - Review user experience in production | Active - Review usability metrics, identify design improvements |
| **Technical Writer** | Consult - Assess documentation needs | Active - Plan documentation scope and timeline | Active - Create and review documentation, collaborate with developers | Active - Finalize and publish documentation | Consult - Review documentation effectiveness, identify gaps |
| **Scrum Master** | Consult - Advise on team capacity and process | Active - Facilitate planning ceremonies, remove early blockers | Lead - Run daily standups, sprint reviews, remove impediments | Active - Ensure smooth sprint closure, coordinate release activities | Lead - Facilitate retrospective, track action items |
| **DevOps Engineer** | Consult - Assess infrastructure and deployment needs | Active - Plan CI/CD pipeline, infrastructure changes, identify deployment risks | Active - Maintain pipelines, support deployments, monitor systems | Lead - Execute deployment, verify infrastructure, monitor production | Active - Review deployment process, propose automation improvements |
| **Support/Customer Success** | Consult - Provide customer insights and pain points | Consult - Review acceptance criteria from customer perspective | Consult - Validate features against customer needs, prepare for rollout | Active - Communicate changes to customers, monitor feedback | Active - Share customer feedback, identify quality issues |

### Legend
- **Lead**: Primary responsibility and decision-making authority for activities in this phase
- **Active**: Regular, substantial involvement with hands-on contributions
- **Consult**: Provides input, feedback, or subject matter expertise as needed

## Role Hand-off Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure clear hand-offs and accountability between roles during key project transitions:

### Initiation → Planning
- [ ] Product Manager has documented problem statement and success metrics
- [ ] Project Manager has created initial project charter and stakeholder list
- [ ] UX Designer has completed preliminary user research (if applicable)
- [ ] DevOps Engineer has assessed infrastructure and deployment requirements
- [ ] Technical Writer has been briefed on documentation scope
- [ ] Support team has provided customer insights and known pain points

### Planning → Execution
- [ ] Backlog is prioritized with clear acceptance criteria (Product Manager)
- [ ] Project plan, timeline, and risk register are established (Project Manager)
- [ ] Design mockups and user flows are approved (UX Designer)
- [ ] CI/CD pipeline and environments are ready (DevOps Engineer)
- [ ] Team has completed sprint planning with capacity confirmed (Scrum Master)
- [ ] Documentation plan is aligned with feature delivery (Technical Writer)

### Execution → Release
- [ ] All acceptance criteria validated and features tested (Developers, QA)
- [ ] Documentation completed and published (Technical Writer)
- [ ] Deployment runbook and rollback plan prepared (DevOps Engineer)
- [ ] Release communication drafted for customers (Product Manager, Support)
- [ ] Production monitoring and alerting configured (DevOps Engineer)
- [ ] Support team trained on new features and changes (Support/Customer Success)

### Release → Retrospective
- [ ] Release deployed successfully and verified in production (DevOps Engineer)
- [ ] Success metrics measured against targets (Product Manager)
- [ ] Customer feedback collected and analyzed (Support/Customer Success)
- [ ] Technical debt and follow-up work identified (Developers, Technical Writer)
- [ ] Retrospective scheduled with all key participants (Scrum Master, Project Manager)
